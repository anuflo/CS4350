CS4350 Game Development Project  

Learning Outcomes

This project-based course offers students the opportunity to work in teams to design and develop a game by following the key stages of the game development process. The course emphasises the design and implementation of core dynamics, game mechanics, player strategy, progression systems, balancing, level design, user interfaces, and technical features such as 3D graphics, animation, artificial intelligence, physics, and networking. For the teams which are continuing the game from CS3247, the focus will be on game balancing, polishing, novel software patterns and additional levels.

Students will apply software engineering principles throughout the development lifecycle. They will also analyse existing games, explore market trends and opportunities, pitch original game concepts, and evaluate designs through structured playtests to understand user needs. Development will follow appropriate software development models and project management methodologies.

In addition to technical skills, the course fosters teamwork, communication, and professional presentation. Students will also learn to build a portfolio to support careers in the game industry.

FINAL EVALUATION CRITERIA (GOLD PHASE):-

  1. Game Design– Concept, Premise, Game Play, Mechanics/Dynamics, Experience/Emotion. The mechanics and dynamics that are crafted to bring the intended emotion. 
  2. Types of Fun element(Hard, Easy, Serious, People) present in the game. A game need not focus on all 4 types of fun element. However, it it focuses on a fun type, it should be done well with some mechanics, themes, technologies, etc and it need not be complex.  
  3. Game Balancing & Flow. Balanced game results in fair gameplay.  Player centric progressive challenges will improve the Flow/Interest. Well-paced educational flow and learning curve aligned with learning objectives.  
  4. Aesthetics (Graphics, Animation and Visuals) - Are they well polished? Explosion particles, reactions after hit, firing impact, etc. Quality, variety, and coherence of AI/PCG-generated visuals.
  5. Sound effects and Music. Are they well polished? - Suitable effects and background music at appropriate timings and they match well with the overall gameplay.
  6. Technical:AI, Physics, Maths, Networking (Multiplayer), Special Techniques for handling UI, VR specific UI/UX design, Distribution channel integration (eg. steam, play store), Use of AI/ML tools for development, Depth and sophistication of AI/ML/Procedural Content Generation or others. Not all aspects are required to present in all games.  
  7. Presentation(in STePS), Promotion & Start-up effort (Website, Video, Poster, Active Sharing in Social Media/blogs by members/Links, Publishing in Game Stores (number of downloads, feedbacks, etc), Competition submission, Research Report, Letter of recognition for social track projects.   

    Track-Specific Notes:

    • Commercial: Market readiness (stability, onboarding, monetization or release polish).

    • Research: Hypothesis clarity, evaluation rigor, and publication readiness.

    • Competition: Compliance with competition rules and submission standards.

    • Social: Measurable social impact (e.g., adoption numbers, testimonials).

Note: The evaluation criteria apply to all themes and tracks. However, depending on your chosen theme and track, some criteria will carry more weight or require different emphasis. For example, teams in the Research Track must demonstrate strong technical innovation and evaluation design, while Commercial Track teams must show market traction and polish.
